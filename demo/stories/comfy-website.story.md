# Comfy.org — Comprehensive User Story

## Who
A potential ComfyUI user — an AI artist, researcher, developer, or hobbyist — who lands on www.comfy.org for the first time through a search engine, social media, or a friend's recommendation. They want to understand what ComfyUI is, why it matters, and how to get started.

## Goal
Explore every section of the comfy.org landing page in depth — from the hero section through features, download options, Comfy Cloud, news, open-source philosophy, community gallery, social links, and careers — to fully understand the product and ecosystem before downloading.

## Happy path

### Chapter 1 — First impressions & hero
1. **Land on the home page** — The title reads "ComfyUI | Generate video, images, 3D, audio with AI". The hero section is visually striking and immediately communicates what ComfyUI does.
2. **Notice the branding** — The ComfyUI logo anchors the top-left. Clean, modern, professional.
3. **Read the hero headline** — "The most powerful tool for generative AI, and it's open source." One sentence that captures the entire value proposition.
4. **See the navigation bar** — Across the top: Products, Community NEW, Resources, About, Pricing, Enterprise, Download, Comfy Cloud. A full ecosystem.
5. **Spot the call-to-action** — Download and Get Started buttons invite new users to jump right in.
6. **Absorb the hero visuals** — Large screenshots or animations showing the node-based interface in action.

### Chapter 2 — The power statement
7. **Scroll to "most powerful tool" section** — The page reinforces why ComfyUI is different from other AI tools.
8. **Read the supporting copy** — It generates video, images, 3D, and audio — not just images.
9. **Notice the breadth of capabilities** — This isn't a single-purpose tool; it's a full creative platform.
10. **See the visual examples** — Screenshots or renders showing the diversity of what ComfyUI can produce.

### Chapter 3 — Download ComfyUI
11. **Reach the Download section** — A prominent "Download ComfyUI" heading makes it impossible to miss.
12. **See platform options** — Download buttons for Windows, Mac, Linux — whatever your OS.
13. **Notice the simplicity** — One click to download, no complicated setup instructions on the landing page.
14. **Understand the value** — Free, open-source, runs locally on your own hardware.

### Chapter 4 — Comfy Cloud
15. **Scroll to the Comfy Cloud section** — For users who don't have powerful GPUs or want cloud rendering.
16. **Read the Cloud pitch** — Run workflows in the cloud without managing infrastructure.
17. **Notice the contrast** — Local vs. cloud — ComfyUI gives you both options.
18. **See the Cloud CTA** — Links to try Comfy Cloud for GPU-limited users.

### Chapter 5 — Latest News & Updates
19. **Reach the news section** — "Latest News & Updates" keeps users informed about releases.
20. **Browse the news cards** — Blog posts, release notes, community highlights.
21. **Notice the recency** — Active development signals a healthy, maintained project.
22. **Appreciate the transparency** — Public roadmap, open development, community-driven features.

### Chapter 6 — Designed for Control and Creativity
23. **Scroll to the features section** — "Designed for Control and Creativity" is the core product pitch.
24. **Full Control with Nodes** — The node-based interface gives you complete control over every step of generation.
25. **Reusable Workflows** — Save, share, and remix workflows — no need to rebuild from scratch.
26. **Live Preview** — See results in real time as you build and tweak your workflow.
27. **Appreciate the design philosophy** — Power users get granular control; beginners can use shared workflows.

### Chapter 7 — Open Source and Yours to Shape
28. **Reach the open-source section** — ComfyUI is open source and community-driven.
29. **Read the philosophy** — "Open Source and Yours to Shape" — it's your tool, not a rented service.
30. **Notice the GitHub link** — The source code is public, auditable, and forkable.
31. **Understand the implications** — No vendor lock-in, no subscription required, community contributions welcome.

### Chapter 8 — Trusted by teams
32. **See the social proof** — "Trusted by teams at" followed by company logos or names.
33. **Read the team names** — Professional teams and organizations using ComfyUI in production.
