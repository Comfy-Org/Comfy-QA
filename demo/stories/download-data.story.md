# Download Statistics — User Story

## Who
A custom node author who wants to track their package's growth, compare adoption against competitors, and understand which tools the community trusts most.

## Goal
Check download trends for their own package and see how it stacks up against other nodes in the ecosystem — all from a single dashboard.

## Happy path

1. **Land on the dashboard** — Open the download statistics page. Headline metrics immediately show total ecosystem downloads, package count, and active maintenance stats.
2. **Read the big picture** — The summary counters give an instant sense of ecosystem scale — how many packages exist and how many people are downloading them.
3. **Explore the trend chart** — An interactive chart shows download volume over time. Hover to inspect daily numbers and spot release-day spikes or sustained growth.
4. **Scroll to the leaderboard** — Packages are ranked by total downloads. The top entries are the most trusted, battle-tested tools in the community.
5. **Inspect individual rows** — Each row shows package name, author, and download count — a quick way to evaluate maturity at a glance.
6. **Discover the long tail** — Keep scrolling to find smaller, niche packages. Some of the best tools hide further down the list.
7. **Return to overview** — Scroll back to the top for a final summary before closing.

## Key features to demo
- Headline metrics (total downloads, package count, active packages)
- Interactive download trend chart with hover tooltips
- Ranked leaderboard of packages by downloads
- Per-row detail: package name, author, download count
- Long-tail discovery by scrolling deeper

## Implicit narration (what the voiceover should explain)
- WHY tracking downloads matters (validates your work, informs tool choices)
- HOW the chart reveals trends (release spikes, sustained adoption)
- WHAT the leaderboard tells you (community trust, battle-tested vs new)
- WHERE hidden gems live (the long tail of the ecosystem)

## Out of scope for this demo
- Filtering by date range or category (not available on the dashboard)
- Publishing or managing your own package (that's the registry demo)
- Comparing multiple packages side by side
